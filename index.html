Yoga benefits beyond the mat

Better body image

Yoga cultivates self-awareness. It directs your attention to the capabilities of your body right now. It aids in the development of both physical and mental strength. It has nothing to do with looks.

Becoming a healthy eater

Yoga increases your awareness of your body's sensations. You can continue to practice this increased awareness during mealtimes by taking note of the flavors, textures, and odors of the food as you savor each bite or sip.

Weightloss boost and maintenance

Researchers discovered that those who did yoga for at least 30 minutes once a week for four years or more experienced a decrease in weight gain during middle age. Overweight individuals actually dropped weight. In general, people who practiced yoga had lower body mass indices (BMIs) than people who didn't. This was linked by researchers to mindfulness. A more positive relationship with food and eating can result from mindful eating.

Heart benefits

Yoga can help to lower blood pressure, cholesterol level and blood sugar. It can help people with depression and arthritis.
